On July 17, 1958, the Commission entered an interlocutory order determing that the

claimant Nooksack Tribe was entitled to an award in the sum of $52,383.50, less such offsets

as may be allowable under the Indian Claims Commission Act. The defendant on August 11, 1958,
filed a motion for rehearing and brief in support thereof. The plaintiff on August 18, 1958,
likewise filed a motion for rehearing and brief in support thereof. Thereafter, on October 6,
1958, oral argument was had on said motions for rehearing.. Thereafter, on October 9, 1958, the
defendant filed a supplemental motion for rehearing, and plaintiff, on October 9, 1985, filed
its reply to said supplemental motion for rehearing. The Commission having considered said
motions and arguments, and having had the same under advisement and in accordance with the
opinion filed this date finds that said motions for rehearing should be denied.

IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED, That said motions for rehearing of claimant and defendant be, and
the same are, hereby denied.

Further, the Commission, on its own motion, and for the purpose of clarifying Finding of
Fact No. 35 filed as part of its interlocutory order entered in this case on July 17, 1958,
orders that said Finding 35 be stricken and there shall be set forth in lieu thereof:

35. Upon the basis of the record and the facts as found above, the Commission

finds that the Nooksack tract, consisting of 80,590 acres, had a fair market value
as of March 8, 1859, of $52,383.50, or an average per acre value of $0.65 for the

entire tract.
