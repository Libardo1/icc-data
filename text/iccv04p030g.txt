INTERLOCUTORY DEGREE GRANTING PARTIAL SUMMARY JUDGMENT

 

Upon consideration of petitioners‘ joint motion for summary
judgment on certain issues raised by the respective pleadings
respecting the effect of the Treaty of Fort Laramie, September 17,
1851 (10 Stat. 749) as respects petitioners’ claim that the said
treaty recognized their title to the territory described therein
and defendant's allegation that the said treaty was a treaty of
peace and amity only; and the defendant having objected to the
petitioners’ motion, and the Commission having considered such
objections and certain documentary exhibits offered in support of
petitioners’ motion and having concluded thercfrem that there was
no substantial dispute as to any question of fact, and having made
findings of fact based thereon,

IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED, That petitioners‘ motion for partial
summary judgment be, and the same is hereby granted; and it is

further

ADJUDGED AND DECREED, That the Treaty of Fort Laramie, Septemv
ber 17, 1851 (10 Stat. 749) as respects petitioners‘ claim to the
territory described therein, was not merely a treaty of peace and
amity and an agreement between the Indian tribes themselves, but
